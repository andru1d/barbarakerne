teen's corner
Being a teenager in the digital age can be difficult. Adults saying this does not help. What do adults know about what you are going through in middle and high school in the social media world? Your parents may even seem to cause some problems in the first place.
Teen years are tricky. On the one hand you want personal freedom and autonomy. Meanwhile, you are navigating tough life decisions. You could use some help!
Here is a sample of teen and early adult dilemmas:
  • Should I go to college? If I don't, what life path will I take?
  • My friends are taking drugs. Should I try them, too? Should I dump the friends?
  • Should I put that picture up or make that statement on Facebook? Will it cause me to lose friends? Self-esteem? Job opportunities in the future?
  • How can I get help when my grades are falling?
    How can I keep my freedom and independence?
  • If I have concerns about a friend, how can I ask for my parents' help without them making me stop the friendship?
  • How do I know if I am in a healthy relationship with my boyfriend / girlfriend?
  • What will I do with my life?
Often parents bring teens in for counseling with the plan that I will "fix" them. This is not how I work. It is not what I will ever do as a counselor.
I will ask your parents to work on their relationship with you as much as I will ask you to work on your relationship with them. I will help you define your needs and assist you in communicating them to your parents.
I will help you to navigate and cope with your specific life issues and anxieties. I will help you bridge any communication gap that develops between you and your parents.
My job is to help you and your parents create a healthy and safe relationship that is mutually respectful, as you begin to gain responsibility and establish your independence.
