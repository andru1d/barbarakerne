play therapy
Play therapy is the systematic use of a theoretical model to establish an interpersonal process wherein trained play therapists use the therapeutic powers of play to help clients prevent or resolve psychosocial difficulties and achieve optimal growth and development.
[The Association for Play Therapy]
Play is a natural means for expression and communication for children. Play is directly linked to cognitive development. Play develops childrens' executive functions and emotional regulation.
Play therapy is an intervention, in which children use play to access their natural capacity for self-healing.
In play therapy, children express how they feel about themselves, the significant people in their lives, and significant experiences. I work to examine and work through play themes, revealing each child's life experiences and emotional content, in order to facilitate healing and healthy development. Play also provides the child with a safe psychological distance for children to confront even the most serious problems.
A play therapist is a mental health professional trained to assess, interpret, and utilize children's play to help reduce or eliminate symptomatic behaviors and optimize developmental functioning. Play therapy addresses: depression, ADHD, impulsiveness, conduct disorders, like bullying, aggression, attachment issues and disorder, anxiety and fearfulness, low self-esteem, grief, abuse and other traumatic life experiences, reading difficulties, and other school problems.
